TagTeamSigns Redesign

In my User Interface Design I class, we had 10 weeks to choose a website and redesign its homepage and interior page. I chose tagteamsigns.com, a website for the company Tag Team Signs.

What is Tag Team Signs?

Tag Team Signs is a company that designs and creates unique and affordable signs for commercial businesses.

Immediate Goals

  • Counterintuitive Design: single page format + disappearing navigation bar made it difficult to navigate the sections
  • Color Scheme: vibrant red text + background hurts the user's eyes
  • Readability: vibrant red text on top of an image is difficult to read

Sketching Wireframes

To evaluate how I should reorganize the content from one page into multiple pages, I conducted a card sort and created an User Persona.

After deciding on my categories and site mapping, it was time to start sketching my wireframes.

Result

Below are the finished wireframes:

After finalizing my high-resolution prototype, I’m satisfied with the final choices I’ve made. When going to the homepage, the user sees the pink, black, and white color scheme that is reminiscent of the previous logo while still being more readable. The readability is also improved because there is no text on images anymore.

The navigation of the website is more intuitive because I created separate pages for each section of the website. Thus, the user no longer has to scroll as far as the non-sticky navigation bar.

Speaking of the individual pages, the interior page that I created, the Our Work page, now has more content because there are mini descriptions of each product they offer and there is also a tabbed gallery that shows examples of the company’s work.

In conclusion, while the design may not be perfect, I believe that I have achieved the immediate goals that I established in the beginning: readability, navigation, and content.
